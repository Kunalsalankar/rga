DEFECT 1: DUSTY - Surface Dust or Soiling
================================================================================

WHAT THIS FAULT MEANS:
- Description: Surface dust or soiling reduces irradiance reaching cells.
- Typical impact: Gradual power loss; may trigger performance ratio drop without immediate electrical hazard.
- Risk notes: Dust accumulation can mask other surface issues; heavy soiling can lead to localized heating under some operating conditions.
- Confidence level: If confidence > 0.70, proceed with immediate cleaning.

URGENCY LEVEL:
- Urgency: LOW to MEDIUM (Non-critical but affects efficiency)
- Action timeline: Plan cleaning within 1-2 weeks
- Safety risk: Minimal electrical hazard

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (FIRST 15-30 MINUTES):
- First step: Inspect visually to confirm dust extent
- Safety/PPE: Standard work gloves and eye protection
- Safety note: Avoid working during peak sun hours to reduce thermal stress
- Notification: Inform facility manager of cleaning requirement
- Who to notify: Operations team, maintenance supervisor
- Safety considerations: Use insulated gloves if working near electrical components

MAINTENANCE PROCEDURE (SOP-aligned):
- SOP-CLEAN-001 (Routine cleaning)
- Scope: Scheduled or ad-hoc cleaning of modules to remove dust/soiling
- Method: Use deionized water if available; avoid abrasive brushes
- Cleaning method: Spray with water early morning/evening to reduce thermal stress
- Post-check: Confirm no residue remains; visually inspect for cracks or delamination
- Tools/materials: Deionized water, soft brushes, protective gloves, eye protection
- Do-not-do warnings: Do NOT use tap water (mineral deposits); do NOT use abrasive materials
- Post-action verification: Verify no water streaks; check for new defects

DOCUMENTATION & FOLLOW-UP:
- Record: panel_id, timestamp, cleaning performed, visual inspection results
- Photo documentation: Before and after cleaning photos
- Next inspection: Schedule follow-up in 4 weeks

WHAT INFORMATION IS STILL NEEDED:
- Specific dust composition (pollen, sand, industrial particulates)
- Accumulated dust thickness
- Environmental conditions (weather, air quality)

DEFECT 2: BIRD-DROP - Bird Droppings
================================================================================

WHAT THIS FAULT MEANS:
- Description: Bird droppings are opaque and may create small shaded regions.
- Typical impact: Disproportionate power loss due to mismatch losses; can create localized hotspots in affected cells.
- Severity: HIGH (causes significant localized power loss)
- Risk notes: Persistent droppings can increase hotspot likelihood compared to uniform dust.
- Hotspot risk: Can reach cell temperatures exceeding 80Â°C under operating conditions

URGENCY LEVEL:
- Urgency: MEDIUM to HIGH (Priority cleaning needed)
- Action timeline: Clean within 3-5 days to prevent hotspot damage
- Safety risk: Moderate electrical hazard if hotspots develop

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (FIRST 15-30 MINUTES):
- First step: Locate and assess bird-drop extent and location
- Safety/PPE: Gloves, eye protection, respiratory mask if bird-borne disease risk
- Containment note: Isolate affected panel area if possible to prevent spread
- Notification: Inform facility manager and health/safety officer
- Who to notify: Operations team, maintenance supervisor, health officer
- Safety considerations: Use insulated gloves; avoid skin contact; follow biohazard protocol

MAINTENANCE PROCEDURE (SOP-aligned):
- SOP-CLEAN-001 (Routine cleaning)
- Scope: Removal of bird droppings from modules
- Method: Gentle spraying with deionized water and soft cloth
- Cleaning sequence: Start from top, work downward to avoid re-soiling
- Avoid spreading: Use separate cloth for each panel to prevent cross-contamination
- Post-check: Verify complete removal; inspect for damage from droppings
- Tools/materials: Deionized water, soft cloths, gloves, disinfectant wipes
- Do-not-do warnings: Do NOT scrape droppings (may damage glass); do NOT reuse cloths on other panels
- Post-action verification: Thermal image if available to confirm no hotspots

DOCUMENTATION & FOLLOW-UP:
- Record: panel_id, timestamp, bird-drop removal, inspection for damage
- Photo documentation: Before, during, and after cleaning
- Incident reporting: Report to facilities for bird deterrent assessment
- Next inspection: Schedule follow-up in 2 weeks

WHAT INFORMATION IS STILL NEEDED:
- Frequency of bird visitation (isolated incident vs. recurring)
- Bird deterrent measures already in place
- Presence of visible cell damage from droppings

DEFECT 3: PHYSICAL-DAMAGE - Cracks, Breaks, or Frame Damage
================================================================================

WHAT THIS FAULT MEANS:
- Description: Cracks, shattered glass, frame damage, punctures, or broken encapsulation.
- Typical impact: Can lead to moisture ingress and insulation degradation; may cause rapid performance decline.
- Severity: CRITICAL (Safety and performance risk)
- Risk notes: May increase electrical safety risk depending on severity and location.
- Moisture risk: Compromised encapsulation allows water ingress leading to corrosion
- Electrical hazard: Cracked glass can expose electrical paths and create fire risk

URGENCY LEVEL:
- Urgency: CRITICAL (Immediate action required)
- Action timeline: Isolate panel immediately; replacement within 24-48 hours
- Safety risk: HIGH electrical and physical safety hazard

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (FIRST 15-30 MINUTES):
- First step: STOP - Do not touch damaged panel; mark area as hazardous
- Safety/PPE: Safety glasses, work gloves, insulated gloves for electrical safety
- Isolation step: Physically isolate affected panel; tape warning labels
- Notification: Immediately notify facility manager and safety officer
- Who to notify: Operations team, maintenance supervisor, electrical safety officer, management
- Safety considerations: CRITICAL - potential electrical shock hazard; treat as live unless confirmed de-energized

MAINTENANCE PROCEDURE (SOP-aligned):
- SOP-INSPECT-002 (Detailed inspection)
- Trigger: Any suspected Physical-Damage or repeated alerts
- Method: Visual inspection for crack propagation, glass exposure, frame integrity
- Do NOT attempt repair: Physical-damage panels should be replaced
- SOP-ISOLATE-003 (Electrical isolation)
- Trigger: Confirmed Physical-Damage with potential exposure of conductors
- Action: Isolate affected string per site procedure; record string ID and panel location
- Tools/materials: Warning tape, isolation equipment, replacement panel, PPE
- Do-not-do warnings: Do NOT attempt to clean or repair; do NOT touch exposed edges
- Post-action verification: Confirm panel is de-energized before approach

DOCUMENTATION & FOLLOW-UP:
- Record: panel_id, timestamp, damage type, location, severity, isolation status
- Photo documentation: Wide-angle and close-up photos of damage
- Engineering assessment: Document crack length, direction, moisture ingress
- Replacement order: Create work order for panel replacement
- Next inspection: Installation inspection after replacement

WHAT INFORMATION IS STILL NEEDED:
- Exact location of damage (edge, center, under junction box)
- Cause of damage (impact, manufacturing defect, environmental stress)
- Whether frame is affected or just glass/encapsulation

DEFECT 4: ELECTRICAL-DAMAGE - Component or Wiring Damage
================================================================================

WHAT THIS FAULT MEANS:
- Description: Damage to electrical components, wiring, connectors, or cell interconnects; may include burn marks or discoloration.
- Typical impact: Potential risk of circuit interruption, short circuits, or fire hazard; may cause sudden performance loss or safety hazard.
- Severity: CRITICAL (Fire and electrical hazard)
- Risk notes: Requires immediate electrical isolation and professional assessment; may pose safety risk to technicians.
- Fire risk: Burned or discolored components indicate potential overheating or arcing
- Short circuit risk: Damaged insulation can cause ground faults or phase-to-phase shorts

URGENCY LEVEL:
- Urgency: CRITICAL (Immediate isolation required)
- Action timeline: De-energize immediately; professional assessment within 4 hours
- Safety risk: EXTREME - Fire and electrical shock hazard

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (FIRST 15-30 MINUTES):
- First step: IMMEDIATELY stop operation; de-energize the affected string
- Safety/PPE: Insulated gloves (Class 2), safety glasses, FR clothing if available
- De-energization: Switch off the affected inverter string before approaching panel
- Notification: IMMEDIATELY notify facility manager, safety officer, and electrician
- Who to notify: Operations team, maintenance supervisor, electrical safety officer, fire safety officer
- Safety considerations: CRITICAL - Do NOT touch panel or wiring; potential for arc flash; treat as energized unless confirmed

MAINTENANCE PROCEDURE (SOP-aligned):
- SOP-INSPECT-002 (Detailed inspection)
- Trigger: Confirmed Electrical-Damage alert
- Method: Thermographic inspection for hot spots; visual inspection for burn marks
- Insulation testing: Perform insulation resistance test before re-energization
- SOP-ISOLATE-003 (Electrical isolation)
- Trigger: Confirmed damage with safety risk
- Action: Isolate affected string and panel per lockout/tagout procedure
- Procedure: Follow electrical isolation safety protocol; verify de-energized with multimeter
- Tools/materials: Insulated tools, multimeter, thermocamera, test equipment, FR PPE
- Do-not-do warnings: CRITICAL - Do NOT attempt any repair; do NOT re-energize without professional assessment
- Post-action verification: Insulation resistance testing; thermal imaging verification

DOCUMENTATION & FOLLOW-UP:
- Record: panel_id, timestamp, damage type, location, de-energization timestamp
- Photo documentation: Thermal images showing hot spots; photos of burned/discolored areas
- Electrical testing: Insulation resistance values; continuity test results
- Professional assessment: Third-party electrician inspection required
- Incident report: File formal incident/accident report
- Next step: Panel replacement or repair per professional assessment

WHAT INFORMATION IS STILL NEEDED:
- Type of damage (burn, discoloration, corrosion, break)
- Location within panel (junction box, interconnects, edge connectors)
- Whether damage is internal (interconnects) or external (wiring, connectors)

DEFECT 5: CLEAN - No Defects Detected
================================================================================

WHAT THIS FAULT MEANS:
- Description: Panel surface is clean with no visible soiling, bird droppings, or debris.
- Typical impact: Normal power output; no performance degradation expected from surface contamination.
- Status: OPERATIONAL - Panel is functioning optimally
- Risk notes: Clean panels require standard maintenance schedules; no immediate action required.
- Efficiency impact: Panel operating at baseline design efficiency for current weather conditions

URGENCY LEVEL:
- Urgency: NONE - No immediate action required
- Action timeline: Standard maintenance schedule (quarterly or annual)
- Safety risk: None from cleanliness perspective

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (FIRST 15-30 MINUTES):
- First step: No urgent action required; proceed with normal operations
- Monitoring note: Continue standard monitoring and inspection schedule
- Verification: Confirm power output metrics are normal for current conditions
- Notification: Update inspection log as "Clean - No action required"
- Who to notify: Facility monitoring system; log inspection result
- Safety considerations: Standard operational safety protocols apply

MAINTENANCE PROCEDURE (Standard Operation):
- Scope: Continue regular monitoring and preventive maintenance schedule
- Method: No cleaning action needed; focus on electrical performance monitoring
- Monitoring interval: Continue normal SCADA monitoring every 15-30 minutes
- Post-check: Verify IV curve and power output remain within expected ranges
- Tools/materials: Monitoring system, SCADA platform, performance analytics
- Do-not-do warnings: Do NOT neglect other panels; do NOT reduce monitoring frequency
- Post-action verification: Confirm efficiency ratio meets design specifications

DOCUMENTATION & FOLLOW-UP:
- Record: panel_id, timestamp, "Clean" status, power output metrics
- Performance log: Log normal operation efficiency and output
- Next inspection: Schedule routine inspection per standard interval
- Trend analysis: Compare against historical clean panel performance

WHAT INFORMATION IS STILL NEEDED:
- Comparative power output vs. design specifications
- Weather conditions during inspection
- Time since last cleaning cycle

DEFECT 6: SNOW-COVERED - Snow, Ice, or Frost Accumulation
================================================================================

WHAT THIS FAULT MEANS:
- Description: Panel surface covered with snow, ice, or frost accumulation.
- Typical impact: Complete or near-complete blockage of light; power output severely reduced during winter/cold conditions.
- Severity: HIGH (Temporary but severe power loss)
- Risk notes: May clear naturally or require supervised removal; risk of thermal shock if warm water used for melting; avalanche risk in high-angle installations.
- Production impact: 80-100% power loss while covered; 30-50% loss even with partial coverage
- Duration: May last hours to days depending on weather conditions

URGENCY LEVEL:
- Urgency: MEDIUM (Temporary but severe degradation)
- Action timeline: Monitor natural clearing; manual removal if forecast shows extended coverage
- Safety risk: Moderate to high (avalanche risk on steep roofs; thermal shock hazard)

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (FIRST 15-30 MINUTES):
- First step: Monitor weather forecast to predict natural clearing timeline
- Assessment: Check if melting is naturally occurring or additional accumulation expected
- Notification: Inform facility manager of power loss; update performance forecast
- Who to notify: Operations team, weather monitoring system, facility manager
- Safety note: Do NOT attempt removal during snow/sleet conditions
- Safety considerations: Winter/cold weather hazards; fall hazard on pitched roofs; avalanche risk

MAINTENANCE PROCEDURE (SOP-aligned):
- Assessment phase: Check weather forecast for next 24-48 hours
- Natural clearing: Allow weather-driven melting if temperatures rising
- Active removal (if needed): SOP-SNOW-004 (Snow removal procedure)
- Method: Soft bristle broom or low-pressure water spray (late afternoon/early evening)
- Timing: Only remove during warming conditions to minimize thermal shock risk
- Removal sequence: Work from top to bottom; allow water to drain
- Tools/materials: Soft broom, low-pressure sprayer, thermometer, safety rope/harness
- Do-not-do warnings: CRITICAL - Do NOT use hot water (thermal shock risk); do NOT use pressure washers; do NOT remove on slippery surfaces
- Precautions: Require safety harness on pitched roofs; avalanche watch on steep installations
- Post-action verification: Confirm power output returns to baseline after snow removal

DOCUMENTATION & FOLLOW-UP:
- Record: panel_id, timestamp, snow depth, temperature, action taken
- Weather data: Temperature trend, forecast duration of snow
- Removal log: If manually removed, document removal method and time to recovery
- Next monitoring: Increase monitoring frequency during winter months
- Trend analysis: Track snow coverage patterns for operational planning

WHAT INFORMATION IS STILL NEEDED:
- Snow/ice type (wet heavy snow vs. dry powder vs. ice crust)
- Roof/installation angle (affects removal difficulty and avalanche risk)
- Weather forecast and temperature trend
- Historical snow coverage patterns for location

SECTION B: Company maintenance SOP excerpts (example)

SOP-CLEAN-001 (Routine cleaning)
- Scope: Scheduled or ad-hoc cleaning of modules to remove dust/soiling and bird droppings.
- Method: Use deionized water if available; avoid abrasive brushes; clean early morning/evening to reduce thermal stress.
- Post-check: Confirm no residue remains; visually inspect for cracks or delamination.

SOP-INSPECT-002 (Detailed inspection)
- Trigger: Any suspected Physical-Damage or repeated Bird-drop alerts on same panel.
- Method: Visual inspection for cracks, delamination, frame gaps; check connector integrity.
- Safety: Follow lockout/tagout; use insulated gloves when working near live conductors.

SOP-ISOLATE-003 (Electrical isolation)
- Trigger: Confirmed Physical-Damage with potential exposure of conductors or compromised insulation.
- Action (factual): Isolate affected string per site procedure; record string ID and panel location; notify supervisor.

SECTION C: Decision thresholds (example; informational only)

THRESH-DEC-010 (Model confidence usage)
- If primary confidence is low (e.g., < 0.50), treat prediction as uncertain and prioritize retrieving inspection SOP details.
- If top-2 scores are close (e.g., difference < 0.10), retrieve SOPs covering both defects for human review.

THRESH-CLEAN-011 (Cleaning criteria)
- Dusty or Bird-drop predicted with moderate/high confidence: retrieve cleaning SOP and safe cleaning constraints.
- Repeated Dusty alerts over multiple inspections: retrieve scheduling guidance and cleaning frequency policy.

THRESH-DAMAGE-012 (Damage handling)
- Physical-Damage predicted: retrieve inspection and isolation SOPs and required documentation steps.
